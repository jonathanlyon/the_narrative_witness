Certificate of a Life

for the years no office keeps

Not issued by any registrar · No fee · No appointment · No stranger need be present

Copy 3 of 3 · kept by no office Authored by the bearer

The State certifies that a person was born, and will certify that he died. It keeps no form for the vast, unwitnessed middle where the living actually happens. For most people, family and memory do that filing, invisibly and unpaid. Denied both, the adoptee is left holding the pen. So here is the missing form. No one drafted it. I am issuing it myself.

Part I The life certified
1 · Bearer
Jonathan Lyon
The name I answer to. It will do.

3 · Evidence of life submitted
One book. Braided testimony. Addressed to two readers.

Record of the set · issued in triplicate

Date Entry On file
1967 Born, twice, ten minutes apart, in one upstairs room. Both of us kept our first names. Neither kept the last.
1967 Original birth entry registered, as Jonathan Richard Jordan. True. Complete. Brief.
c.1970 Adoption Order. Both entries sealed; new certificates issued; two boys adopted together. Same date. First and middle names kept, a rare kindness. Only the surname changed.
later Both adoptive parents die. The seal becomes, for the first time, reachable.
later still Sealed copy recovered from Somerset House: counsellor first, then a fee, then a folder of notes and a doctor's report. All since lost in the fog, the moving, the continent after continent.
2026 Certificate of a Life authored and filed nowhere. Witnessed by Ella and Imogen. Window 4 unaffected.
